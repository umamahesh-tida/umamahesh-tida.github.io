Lab News & Announcements

Awards, grants, publications, and milestones from the ACE² Lab.

March 2026

🏛️ Dr. Tida Attended NSF Workshop on Agents for Chip Design Automation

Dr. Tida attended the NSF Workshop on Agents for Chip Design Automation, hosted at UCLA and organized by leading researchers from UCLA, Google, and Columbia. The workshop brought together top experts from academia and industry to explore the rapidly evolving intersection of AI agents and chip design, with the goal of defining a research agenda for NSF funding in this critical area. Topics covered included agentic AI for EDA, LLM-driven RTL generation, AI-assisted ASIC design, and autonomous accelerator design.

February 2026

📄 New Preprint: CQ-CiM – Hardware-Aware Embedding for Robust CiM-Based Retrieval

A new preprint introduces CQ-CiM, a hardware-aware embedding shaping method for robust Compute-in-Memory (CiM)-based retrieval. Co-authored with collaborators from Notre Dame, TU Dresden, and others, this work advances CiM architectures for efficient LLM inference on edge hardware. Available on arXiv:2602.20083.

2026

📄 Journal Paper: Comparative Study of U-Net and YOLOv11 for Weed Segmentation

A comparative study of U-Net and YOLOv11 for weed segmentation has been published in Applied Engineering in Agriculture. This work contributes to AI-driven precision agriculture by benchmarking deep learning segmentation models for real-world weed identification tasks.

June 2025

🌐 NSF IRES Award for International Research Experience

Dr. Tida received an NSF IRES grant as Co-PI, supporting international research experiences on RFID Sensing Systems with Machine Learning. This grant provides students with the opportunity to engage in cutting-edge international research collaborations in advanced sensing and hardware design methodologies.

April 2025

📄 New Preprint: ComplexVCoder – LLM Framework for Complex Verilog Generation

A new preprint presents ComplexVCoder, an LLM-driven framework for the systematic generation of complex Verilog RTL code. The work achieves state-of-the-art Pass@1/Pass@5 performance for hardware description language generation. Available on arXiv:2504.20653.

February 2025

🏆 NSF CAREER Award Received by Dr. Tida

We are proud to announce that Dr. Umamaheswara Rao Tida has received the prestigious NSF CAREER Award — $553,560 over 5 years (2025–2030) — to support research on advanced process technology-based EDA frameworks for efficient Monolithic 3D Integration. This award was received on the very first attempt, reflecting the transformative potential and rigor of the proposed research.

2025

📄 ISQED 2025: ML-Assisted Magnetic-Core Coupled Inductor Design

A paper on machine learning-assisted design of magnetic-core coupled inductors for interleaved buck converters was presented at the 26th International Symposium on Quality Electronic Design (ISQED 2025). The work integrates ML with MINLP solver optimization for on-chip power delivery design.

January 2025

📄 PLoS ONE: Automating Amino Acid Identification with Machine Learning

A paper on automating amino acid identification in elliptical dichroism spectrometers using machine learning has been published in PLoS ONE (Vol. 20, e0317130). This work demonstrates the broader impact of ML in biochemical sensing and characterization applications.

2024

📄 IEEE Access: Modeling and Design of Dual-Purpose MIV in Monolithic 3D IC

A journal paper on the modeling and design of dual-purpose Metal Inter-Layer Vias (MIVs) in Monolithic 3D ICs has been published in IEEE Access (Vol. 12, pp. 23066–23080). The work shows how MIVs can serve dual roles for both on-chip memory and logic connectivity, enabling area reduction in M3D-IC designs.

2024

📄 ISVLSI 2024: Two Papers on Monolithic 3D Integration

Two papers were presented at the 2024 IEEE Computer Society Annual Symposium on VLSI (ISVLSI): a compact 6T-SRAM design leveraging bottom-gate transistors in FD-SOI process for M3D Integration, and a study on design approaches and considerations for reliable and efficient Monolithic 3D Integration.

2024

📄 ASABE 2024: Weed Identification Using U-Net and SAM Segmentation

A paper on weed identification using U-Net and SAM (Segment Anything Model) segmentation was presented at the 2024 ASABE Annual International Meeting, advancing AI-driven precision agriculture tools for herbicide-resistant weed control.
